Deep Dive

Every project starts with deep brand research — reading a brand's visual DNA, tone, and unspoken rules — before a single brief is written.

An editorial campaign for Typology — the French skincare brand built on radical ingredient transparency. Created entirely with AI generation tools. Every image was directed from brief to final frame with no physical shoot. This project demonstrates how AI visual production can extend a brand's creative language beyond what's been photographed — into what's still possible.

The goal was not to imitate Typology's existing aesthetic — it was to extend it. To find the images that could exist inside the brand's logic, but haven't been made yet.

Typology is a brand built on radical transparency — ingredients listed, percentages declared, no narrative excess. Its design language is minimal to the point of philosophy. This is a brand that trusts the product to speak.

Before generating a single image, I spent time inside Typology's visual world — its website, its Instagram grid, its packaging system. What I found: Materiality over beauty. Typology photographs product as object. The aluminum tube is not glamorized — it is presented as it is, with industrial honesty. Skin as surface, not ideal. Their campaign portraits show freckles, pores, uneven tone. The face is a record, not a correction.

The hand is Typology's most recurring motif. It holds, squeezes, presents — but never performs. It is always functional, never decorative. I built a sub-series around this observation. Each frame studies the relationship between the hand and the product as a quiet transaction: the transfer of formula from aluminum to skin, from concept to care.

The macro shots of cream texture — swirled, smeared, spread — function as abstract images within a beauty campaign. They are ingredient portraits.

Typology names its products like laboratory references — A90, L14, R51. Clinical codes that strip sentiment from function. I wanted to take that logic and push it until it broke — beautifully.

"A90. Served." places the nourishing hand cream on a concrete plate, squeezed flat by cutlery, cap detached, cream escaping at the bottom. It is treated like food. Like a dish. Like something to be consumed. The reading is immediate: this cream is so good you could eat it. But the visual says something stranger — that care is a ritual, that the body requires nourishment the same way the table does. This is the campaign's statement image. The one that stops the scroll.

The T40 Sheer Honey Lip Oil required a different register entirely. Honey is not clinical. Honey is ancient, sticky, alive. A bee doesn't care about percentages.

I built two frames in tension with each other: one cold and scientific (product held upright, honey dripping, a single bee landed on the cap — impossible, but physically plausible); one viscerally human (honeycomb between lips, gold honey falling from the lower lip in long strings). Neither image shows the product being applied. Both images make you reach for it. This is the campaign's sensory axis — where Typology's ingredient transparency becomes something you can almost taste.

The Typology soap bar presented a different creative problem: a product without decoration, without color, without graphic complexity. A white rectangle with embossed text. This is a test. When the object has nothing to hide behind, the frame must do the work.

Three directions explored: The coil. The shower hose becomes a graphic element — industrial, circular, grounding the bar within its functional context. The image is about where this object lives. The rupture. A hand breaks through torn paper to present the bar — a gesture of revelation, of something emerging. The bar as discovery. The ground. Bar centered on a steel hose spiral, table-level view, warm shadow. The quietest frame. Confidence through simplicity.

One frame. One decision: let the impossible happen. The shadow cast on the wall behind the bottle reads the label back to you — every line of text, perfectly legible, projected in darkness. A shadow that knows too much. Physically impossible. Visually inevitable.

This is the image's single surreal gesture — quiet enough that you almost accept it. The kind of detail that rewards a second look, then a third. Why can I read the ingredients in the shadow? Because in this campaign, transparency is literal. Typology declares what's inside. Here, the shadow declares it too.

The rest of the frame holds discipline: near-monochrome, deep sepia tone, the hand barely distinct from its own cast shape. The bottle disappears into darkness and remains completely present. Two things at once — which is exactly what a good exfoliant does to the skin. No color. No softness. No explanation. Just light, and what it chooses to reveal.

The R51 Repairing Hair Mask — ceramides and avocado oil — gave me the most surreal territory in the campaign. The avocado is stitched back together with white cord, sutured like a wound, the product tube threaded through the cavity where the pit once sat. It is repair made literal. The same logic that rebuilds hair structure (ceramide = the body's own architecture) is applied to the fruit.

The standalone image — tube twisted, cinched at the waist with white cord lacing, standing upright like a figure — turns the packaging itself into sculpture. An object made strange by what's been done to it.

Massimo Dutti — AI Fashion Campaign & Art Direction

Neutral Harmony SS26

VISUAL DIRECTION

This campaign reinterprets Massimo Dutti's signature minimalism through the lens of brutalist architecture and soft neutral textures. Linen blend knit and organically grown cotton become sculptural elements, while light, shadow and reflection create a quiet dialogue between body and space.

AI was the camera. The vision was human.

Architectural forms & knit textures

Inspired by Massimo Dutti's timeless elegance, the campaign explores the harmony between raw architectural forms and delicate knit textures. The color palette — warm beige, stone grey and soft sand — creates a monochromatic world where clothing and environment become one.

ART DIRECTION

Production pipeline

Building a stable AI model requires a disciplined creative approach. This ensures visual continuity and a fluid shift from the conceptual stage to the animation phase.

From first prompt to final frame — every step was intentionally designed:

  • GENERATE — Face development (Midjourney + Nano Banana)
  • POSE BOARD — Pose & angle exploration (Nano Banana + Flux)
  • ITERATE — Full-body compositions in location (Seedream + Nano Banana)
  • UPSCALE — Maximum detail & resolution (Magnific)
  • GRADE — Cinematic color and light (Lightroom)
  • ANIMATE — Subtle motion (Kling)
